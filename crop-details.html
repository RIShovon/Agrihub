Featured Crops

Tomato

Vegetable

Tomatoes are one of the most popular vegetables grown in home gardens. They are rich in vitamins A and C, and lycopene, an antioxidant that has been linked to many health benefits.

Quick Facts
Scientific Name: Solanum lycopersicum
Growth Duration: 60-100 days
Optimal pH: 6.0-6.8
Water Needs: Moderate to high
Nutritional Value (per 100g)
Calories: 18 kcal
Carbohydrates: 3.9g
Protein: 0.9g
Vitamin C: 14mg
Potassium: 237mg
Cultivation Guide
Soil Preparation

Tomatoes prefer well-drained, fertile soil with plenty of organic matter. Prepare beds by adding compost or well-rotted manure.

Planting

Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before last frost. Transplant seedlings when they have 4-6 true leaves and soil temperature is above 60Â°F.

Spacing

Space plants 24-36 inches apart in rows 36-48 inches apart. Provide support with stakes or cages.

Harvesting & Storage
When to Harvest

Harvest when fruits are fully colored but still firm. Tomatoes will continue to ripen after picking.

Storage

Store at room temperature, not in refrigerator. Use within 5-7 days of harvesting.

Yield

Average yield is 8-10 pounds per plant under good growing conditions.

Rice

Grain

Rice is a staple food for more than half of the world's population. It is cultivated in flooded fields called paddies and requires specific growing conditions.

Quick Facts
Scientific Name: Oryza sativa
Growth Duration: 3-6 months
Optimal pH: 5.5-6.5
Water Needs: High (flooded conditions)
Nutritional Value (per 100g cooked)
Calories: 130 kcal
Carbohydrates: 28g
Protein: 2.7g
Fiber: 0.4g
Iron: 1.5mg
Cultivation Guide
Land Preparation

Prepare paddy fields by plowing and leveling. Ensure proper water control with bunds and irrigation channels.

Planting

Transplant 25-30 day old seedlings into flooded fields. Maintain 2-3 inches of water during early growth.

Water Management

Keep fields flooded until 2-3 weeks before harvest. Then drain to allow grain maturation.

Harvesting & Processing
When to Harvest

Harvest when 80-85% of panicles turn golden yellow and grains are hard.

Processing

Thresh, dry, and mill rice to remove husk and bran layers. Proper drying is crucial to prevent mold.

Yield

Average yield is 4-6 tons per hectare for modern varieties.
