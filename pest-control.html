Pest Control Strategies

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) strategies including prevention, biological controls, and organic remedies

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) is an ecosystem-based strategy that focuses on long-term prevention of pests or their damage through a combination of techniques such as biological control, habitat manipulation, and use of resistant varieties.

IPM Principles

Monitor & Identify

Regular monitoring and accurate pest identification

Prevention

Cultural practices to prevent pest establishment

Control

Least-risk methods when intervention needed

Evaluate

Assess effectiveness and adjust strategies

Common Agricultural Pests

Aphids

Sucking Insect High Risk

Aphids are small, soft-bodied insects that suck sap from plants, causing stunted growth, curled leaves, and reduced yields. They also secrete honeydew that promotes sooty mold growth.

Identification
Size: 1-3 mm long
Color: Green, black, yellow, or pink
Damage Signs: Curled leaves, sticky residue
Affected Crops: Most vegetables and ornamentals
Control Methods
Biological Ladybugs & Lacewings: Release beneficial insects that prey on aphids
Organic Neem Oil Spray: Apply every 7-10 days to disrupt growth
Cultural Companion Planting: Plant garlic, chives, or marigolds as repellents
Prevention Tips
Use reflective mulches to deter aphids
Encourage natural predators with diverse planting
Remove weeds that host aphids
Treatment Schedule
Infestation Level Action Required
Low (few colonies) Monitor weekly, use water spray
Medium (multiple plants) Apply neem oil, introduce predators
High (widespread) Insecticidal soap, remove heavily infested plants

Tomato Hornworm

Chewing Insect Medium Risk

Large green caterpillars that can quickly defoliate tomato plants. They are the larval stage of the five-spotted hawk moth and can cause significant damage if not controlled.

Identification
Size: Up to 4 inches long
Color: Bright green with white markings
Damage Signs: Missing leaves, black droppings
Affected Crops: Tomatoes, peppers, eggplants
Control Methods
Manual Hand Picking: Remove caterpillars by hand (wear gloves)
Biological BT (Bacillus thuringiensis): Apply bacterial spray
Cultural Till Soil: Disrupt overwintering pupae in soil

Organic Pest Control Recipes

Garlic Oil Spray
Target Pests:

Aphids, mites, whiteflies

Ingredients:
  • 10 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon mineral oil
  • 1 pint water
  • 1 teaspoon liquid soap
Instructions:

Soak garlic in mineral oil for 24 hours. Add water and soap. Strain and spray on affected plants.

Neem Oil Solution
Target Pests:

Most soft-bodied insects, fungi

Ingredients:
  • 2 teaspoons neem oil
  • 1 teaspoon liquid soap
  • 1 quart warm water
Instructions:

Mix soap and water first, then add neem oil. Shake well and spray every 7-14 days.

Chili Pepper Spray
Target Pests:

Mammals, chewing insects

Ingredients:
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 6 drops dish soap
  • 1 quart water
Instructions:

Mix all ingredients and let sit overnight. Strain and spray on plant leaves.

Beneficial Insects for Natural Control

Ladybugs

Each ladybug can consume up to 50 aphids per day. They also eat mites, scale insects, and other soft-bodied pests.

How to Attract:
  • Plant dill, fennel, and yarrow
  • Provide shallow water sources
  • Avoid broad-spectrum pesticides
Praying Mantis

Excellent general predators that feed on a wide variety of garden pests including caterpillars, beetles, and grasshoppers.

How to Attract:
  • Provide tall grasses and shrubs for habitat
  • Plant cosmos, marigolds, and dill
  • Leave some areas of garden undisturbed
